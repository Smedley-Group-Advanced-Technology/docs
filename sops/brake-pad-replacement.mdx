## Overview

### Description

This process establishes a standardised method for replacing Kart brake pads.

### Why?

Inconsistency in approach to the fitment and adjustment of brake pads.

### Who?

- The **Chief Mechanic** is responsible for the creation and delivery of this SOP.
- All mechanics, both on and off event, must be made aware of this SOP.

### When?

| Context | Trigger |
| --- | --- |
| Pre-Event (Factory or Track) | Whenever brake pads require replacement |
| In-Event | Whenever brake pads require replacement |
| Post-Event (Factory or Track) | Whenever brake pads require replacement |

## Procedure: Bambino Karts

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm Replacement Required">
    Replacement of brake pads has been determined following the Brake Pad Indicator SOP.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove Split Pin or Clip">
    Remove the split pin or clip retaining the pad slider pin.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove Pad Slider Pin">
    Remove the pad slider pin.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Counter-Hold and Loosen Lock Nut">
    Counter-hold the pad adjustment screw with a 4mm Allen key. While doing so, undo the 10mm K locking nut anti-clockwise.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Retract Piston">
    Undo the pad adjustment screws anti-clockwise until the piston is retracted into the caliper.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove Old Brake Pads">
    Remove old brake pads and dispose of them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Clean Caliper">
    Clean dust and debris from the caliper, paying particular attention to the pad mating surfaces.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Refit New Pads">
    Refit new pads in the correct orientation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Refit Slider Pin">
    Refit the pad slider pin and securing clip.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Air Gap">
    Place the Air Gap Tool between the pad friction surface and the disc face.

    <Note>
      **Outstanding:** A photograph of the Air Gap Tool is required here. Tool number TBC.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Adjust Pad">
    Wind in the pad adjustment screw until slight resistance is felt.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tighten Lock Nut">
    While counter-holding the adjustment screw, tighten the locking K nut clockwise.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Repeat for Opposing Pad">
    Repeat steps 10–12 for the opposing pad.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Operation">
    Check operation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Procedure: Cadet Karts — RR I25 Caliper

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm Replacement Required">
    Replacement of brake pads has been determined following the Brake Pad Indicator SOP.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove Slider Pin Securing Clip">
    Remove the brake pad slider pin securing clip.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove Grub Screw">
    Undo and remove the 5mm grub screw from the slider pin collar and dispose of it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove Slider Pin and Collar">
    Remove the brake pad slider pin and collar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Extract Worn Pads">
    Extract worn brake pads and dispose of them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Retract Pistons if Projected">
    Study the position of the brake pistons in relation to the caliper. If projected, undo the brake caliper bleed screw anti-clockwise 3 turns and push the pistons back into the caliper, capturing the escaping fluid in a suitable vessel.

    <Note>
      **Outstanding:** Additional photos are required to illustrate the different piston projection scenarios and clarify what "projected" looks like.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Retighten Bleed Screw">
    Retighten the caliper bleed screw clockwise.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Clean Caliper">
    Clean dust and debris from the caliper, paying particular attention to the pad mating surfaces.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Refit New Pads">
    Refit new pads in the correct orientation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Refit Slider Pin and Collar">
    Refit the pad slider pin and collar, securing the slider pin with the clip.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fit New Grub Screw">
    Using a new locking 5mm grub screw, tighten the collar to the pin, centring it equally between the two pads.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Operation">
    Check operation. If the brake pedal has excessive travel, proceed to bleed the system of air.

    <Note>
      **Outstanding:** More specific guidelines are needed on what constitutes acceptable versus excessive brake pedal travel.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Procedure: Cadet Karts — CX 124 Caliper

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm Replacement Required">
    Replacement of brake pads has been determined following the Brake Pad Indicator SOP.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Clean Caliper">
    Clean the caliper with brake cleaner, ensuring it is free of dust and debris.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove Slider Pin Securing Clip">
    Remove the brake pad slider pin securing clip.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove Slider Pin">
    Remove the brake pad slider pin.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove Pad Retaining Screws">
    Undo the 4 × M5 CHS pad retaining screws anti-clockwise.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove Old Pads">
    Remove old brake pads and dispose of them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Retract Piston">
    Undo the 3mm brake caliper bleed screw and retract the piston.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Refit Bleed Screw">
    Refit the brake caliper bleed screw.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Refit Brake Pads">
    Refit brake pads into the caliper arms using 4 × new M5 CHS screws.

    <Warning>
      **Outstanding:** Torque specification for M5 CHS pad retaining screws is TBC.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Refit Slider Pin">
    Refit the brake pad slider pin.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Refit Securing Clip">
    Refit the brake pad slider pin securing clip.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Operation">
    Check operation. If the brake pedal has excessive travel, proceed to bleed the system of air.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Procedure: Junior Karts

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm Replacement Required">
    Replacement of brake pads has been determined following the Brake Pad Indicator SOP.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove Securing Clips">
    Remove the brake pad slider pin securing clips.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove Grub Screws">
    Undo and remove the 5mm grub screws from the slider pin collars and dispose of them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove Slider Pins and Collars">
    Remove the brake pad slider pins and collars.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Extract Worn Pads">
    Extract worn brake pads and dispose of them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Retract Pistons if Projected">
    Study the position of the brake pistons in relation to the caliper. If projected, undo the brake caliper bleed screw anti-clockwise 3 turns and push the pistons back into the caliper, capturing the escaping fluid in a suitable vessel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Retighten Bleed Screw">
    Retighten the caliper bleed screw clockwise.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Clean Caliper">
    Clean dust and debris from the caliper, paying particular attention to the pad mating surfaces.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Refit New Pads">
    Refit new pads in the correct orientation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Refit Slider Pins and Collars">
    Refit the pad slider pins and collars, securing the slider pins with clips.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fit New Grub Screws">
    Using new locking 5mm grub screws, tighten the collars to the pins, centring each equally between the two pads.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Top Up Brake Fluid">
    Remove the brake fluid reservoir cap and top up fluid to between the MIN and MAX marks. Replace the reservoir cap.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Operation">
    Check operation. If the brake pedal has excessive travel, proceed to bleed the system of air.

    <Note>
      **Outstanding:** More specific guidelines are needed on what constitutes acceptable versus excessive brake pedal travel.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>
